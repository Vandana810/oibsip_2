Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Whether it was his career in science or politics,APJ Abdul Kalam, former President of India, inspired people across the world. Fondly called India's 'Missile man', Dr Kalam was particularly closed to young people and he loved to be among students.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, at Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu. His life as a student was very challenging, filled with hardships and struggles. There was a time when he had to sell newspapers from door to door to support his family and education.

But his commitment towards education was such that he overcame all hardships and achieved excellence not only in academics; but also held the highest constitutional post in India. He will remain as a role model for generations to come.

1931 : Born

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born to Jainulabdeen, a boat owner, and homemaker Ashiamma.He went to Ramanathapuram Schwartz Matriculation School and worked from an early age to supplement the family

1980-1990 : Development of Prithvi and Agni

Developed Prithvi and Agni missiles. As the chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, he is credited with the development and operationalisation of Agni and Prithvi missiles.

1999 : Autobiography published

'Wings of Fire' is an autobiography of Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam. A well composed novel which revolves around the life of Mr. Kalam ,a renowned scientist ,as he shares his experiences and the minutest of details of his life.

2002-2007 : Elected as Preseident

Kalam was elected as 11th President of India succeeding KR Narayanan. He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884. During his five years in office, he was fondly called ‘The People’s President

2012 : Programme for Youth

Kalam launched the ‘What Can I Give Movement’, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption.Main objective was to spread the message and idea of 'giving' among the youth.

July 27, 2015 : Death

Passed away in Shillong, Meghalaya at age of 83, after collapsing because of a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.His last words, to his aide Srijan Pal Singh, were reportedly: "Funny guy! Are you doing well?"
